uSystolic: Byte-Crawling Unary Systolic Array

Di Wu, Jingjie Li, Zhewen Pan, Younghyun Kim, Joshua San Miguel
June 2022
Cite Project Project Project

Abstract

Brain computer interfaces (BCIs) have been widely adopted to enhance human perception via brain signals with abundant spatial-temporal dynamics, such as electroencephalogram (EEG). In recent years, BCI algorithms are moving from classical feature engineering to emerging deep neural networks (DNNs), allowing to identify the spatial-temporal dynamics with improved accuracy. However, existing BCI architectures are not leveraging such dynamics for hardware efficiency. In this work, we present uBrain, a unary computing BCI architecture for DNN models with cascaded convolutional and recurrent neural networks to achieve high task capability and hardware efficiency. uBrain co-designs the algorithm and hardware, the DNN architecture and the hardware architecture are optimized with customized unary operations and immediate signal processing after sensing, respectively. Experiments show that uBrain, with negligible accuracy loss, surpasses the CPU, systolic array and stochastic computing baselines in on-chip power efficiency by 9.0x, 6.2x and 2.0x.
